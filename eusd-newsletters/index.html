What This Tool Does

EUSD Newsletters is a district-hosted replacement for Smore for creating school newsletters. Staff build a newsletter from titled sections (text, photos, photo galleries, PDF flyers, and links), publish it to a stable web link, and share that link with families through their existing email tool. Every published page is built to be fully accessible (WCAG 2.1 AA), and each newsletter comes with detailed viewership analytics — how many people opened it, how far they read, and which links and flyers they clicked.

Prerequisites

One-Time Setup

  1. An @eusd.org Google account.
  2. Membership in your school's newsletter Google Group (this is what grants you access to your school's newsletters). Ask your site administrator if you are unsure.
  3. A current web browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, or Safari). No install needed.

If You Can't Sign In or Don't See Your School

You are signed in but no schools appear: you have not been added to a school's Google Group yet. Contact your site administrator to be added.

Step-by-Step

The intended workflow once the tool is live:

1 Sign in

Go to https://newsletters.eusd.org and sign in with your EUSD Google account.

2 Pick your school

You'll see the schools you have access to. Choose one.

3 Browse your newsletters and start a new one

After signing in and choosing a school, you land on the Newsletters Home — a visual card grid showing all of the school's newsletters with their status badges and last-edited times. From here:

  • Start a new newsletter — opens a modal where you choose Blank (give it a title and go), Template (a pre-built layout like “Weekly Update” or “Event Announcement”), or Duplicate (pick any past issue to copy its structure).
  • Duplicate last issue — one click to reuse your most recently edited newsletter's section structure as a fresh draft.
  • School analytics — jump to the combined analytics dashboard for all of the school's newsletters.

You can also search newsletters by title, filter by status (Draft / In review / Changes requested / Approved / Published — each pill shows a live count), and sort by last edited, issue date, or title.

Each card has quick actions at the bottom: Edit, Duplicate, and Analytics. The … (more) menu on each card adds Rename, View public page (published newsletters only), and Delete. Deleting shows a 5-second Undo button — the newsletter is not permanently removed until that window closes.

Note: The authoring app (the newsletter list, editor, and dashboards) requires JavaScript in your browser. The public newsletter pages that families read work without it.

4 Customize the banner and build your sections

At the top of the builder, open the Header editor to customize the newsletter banner that families see first:

  • Banner art — pick one of 6 illustrated presets (Waves, Nature, Floral, Fireworks, Confetti, Sunrise). Each preset is also available in a school-color variant (shown as a second swatch beside each preset when your school has a primary color set): the same texture tinted in your school's brand color instead of the preset's default gradient. Choose School color for a clean solid-color banner with no art. Or upload your own photo for a custom full-width banner background — it takes precedence over any preset.
  • School logo — turn the logo on or off per newsletter. When on, choose Small, Medium, or Large for the logo size in the header. (The logo itself is set school-wide under School Branding.)
  • Header text and eyebrow — the headline that families see in the banner. When you create a new newsletter, the header text is pre-filled from the title you entered; edit it any time.

Then add titled sections and drag them into the order you want. In each section add content blocks and reorder them by dragging. Your work autosaves as you go.

Available content block types:

  • Paragraph — formatted text (bold, italic, links).
  • Title — a heading in large, medium, or small size.
  • Text — rich text with bold, italic, bullet lists, and links (the primary text block for most content).
  • Link — a standalone call-to-action link or button.
  • Image — a single photo, optionally floated left or right.
  • Image gallery — 2–4 photos side by side.
  • Flyer — an event flyer, added one of two ways: a typed styled callout (pick one of 7 looks) or by uploading an image of a flyer that the app turns into accessible text beside the picture (see step 5).
  • Event — title, date, time, and location displayed as a structured card.
  • Quote — a highlighted pull-quote with optional attribution.
  • Divider — a visual separator between content areas.
Live now: all block types above; drag-to-reorder for sections and blocks; autosave; and a soft “someone else is editing this section” lock enforced on the server so two people can't overwrite each other.

5 Add photos and flyers

Add an image block (single photo, or wrapped left/right) or an image gallery (2–4 photos across), then use its Upload button to attach an image file (JPG, PNG, GIF, or WebP). Photos are processed automatically — location/camera data is stripped and fast web-sized versions are generated for you.

  • Alt text is required on every image. Type a short description of what the photo shows. Click Generate alt text to get an AI-drafted suggestion — you must read it and accept or edit it; it is never saved automatically.
  • Photo slots are for photos, not text flyers. If you try to upload an image that is really a text-heavy flyer into an image, gallery, or photo-style flyer slot, the app recognizes it and directs you to Flyer → Upload a flyer instead — so the flyer's words become real, screen-reader-friendly text rather than an unreadable picture.

Two ways to add a flyer

The Flyer block can be added two ways:

  • Styled callout — type the event details (title, date, time, location, and an optional button) and pick one of 7 looks (Classic, Calendar, Spotlight, Ticket, Poster, Polaroid, or Ribbon). Every style is built to meet WCAG AA contrast; the photo styles place text over a dark scrim so it stays readable over any image.
  • Upload a flyer — choose Flyer → Upload a flyer and upload an image of a flyer. The app reads it automatically (text and layout) and builds a two-column block: the flyer image on one side (click to enlarge) and the extracted words on the other, rendered as real headings, lists, and links. Any QR codes are turned into real, descriptive links rather than bare web addresses.

After an upload, review and edit everything before publishing: the flyer title, the image description (required), and the extracted text. Choose the layout — image on the left, on the right, or on top of the text. If the flyer comes in two languages, add the Spanish version as an optional second image: both flyers are shown side by side (each click-to-enlarge, the Spanish one labeled), and the reader's translate button handles the words — so you only add the picture, not a second block of text.

Why upload-a-flyer matters: a raw flyer picture is invisible to screen readers. Turning it into structured text beside the image means the page families actually read is fully accessible. An image description is required before you can publish an uploaded flyer.

6 Preview, then submit for review

Before sending your newsletter on, use the Preview button in the editor to see exactly what the public page will look like. When you're ready, click Submit for review. The app runs a full accessibility check first — re-checking every flyer and reviewing every content flag. Submitting is blocked until all Must fix items are resolved and every machine-suggested edit is acknowledged (if the button is greyed out, it explains why). Once submitted, the newsletter goes to your school's approver for sign-off.

7 Approval, then publish

An approver reviews the newsletter and either Approves it or Requests changes with a note back to you. If changes are requested, make the edits and submit it again. Once it is approved, the Publish button becomes available — only an approved newsletter can be published. After publishing, share the newsletter's stable public link with families through ParentSquare, email, or your usual communication tool. To take it offline, mark it back to draft.

Live now: editor Preview, the full review & approval workflow (submit → approve/request changes → publish), the accessibility gate, and the public reader page. See Review & Approval below for the full picture.

8 Watch the analytics

Click View analytics for this newsletter in the editor to see views, reading depth, devices, and which links and flyers families clicked — or View analytics for <school> on the newsletter list for the combined picture across all your issues. See Analytics below for the full picture.

School Branding

Each school can have its own colors and logo. Set these on the School branding screen, reached from the Newsletters Home (it's part of the in-app experience, so it needs JavaScript). A live header preview shows how your choices will look as you make them.

  • Primary color (banner) — used behind the newsletter's header. Title text stays white on a dark scrim over the color, so any color is readable regardless of what you pick.
  • Secondary color (page background) — an optional tint for the page background area behind the newsletter content cards. Leave it blank for a plain white background. This color is not contrast-checked because it sits behind white cards, not behind text.
  • Accent color (links) — used for buttons and links. As you type, the screen shows the color's contrast against white in real time, and the color cannot be saved unless it meets the WCAG AA 4.5:1 threshold. If it falls short, one click applies a suggested darker shade that passes. (The same check runs on the server, so a failing color can never slip through.)
  • Logo — upload a school logo (an image — PNG, JPG, or WebP). It appears immediately in the header preview beside your colors, and you can remove or replace it any time.

Review & Approval

Before a newsletter reaches families, it goes through a short review so a second person signs off on the exact content that will be published. A newsletter moves through these stages:

  • Draft — you're still working on it.
  • In review — you've submitted it and it's waiting for an approver.
  • Changes requested — the approver sent it back with a note; make the edits and submit again.
  • Approved — an approver signed off; it's ready to publish.
  • Published — it's live at its public web address.

For authors: submitting

When your draft is ready, click Submit for review. The app runs the full accessibility check first. Submit (and later Publish) is blocked if there are any unresolved Must fix items or any machine-suggested edits you haven't acknowledged in the Accessibility panel. When that happens the button is disabled and tells you why — open the Accessibility panel and clear the listed items, then submit. If the approver requests changes, you'll see their note; make the fixes and submit again.

Editing sends it back to draft. If you edit a newsletter that has already been approved (or published), it automatically returns to draft and must go through review again. This is on purpose — an approval applies to the exact content that was reviewed, so any later change has to be re-approved.

For approvers: the reviewer screen

You can approve a newsletter if you are a member of your school's approver group, or a district platform administrator. You cannot approve your own newsletter (no self-approval) — someone else has to sign off on it.

Open the reviewer screen from a newsletter's Review action (it's part of the in-app experience). It gathers everything you need in one place:

  • A link to the read-only preview of the page exactly as families will see it.
  • The previous reviewer note, if the newsletter has been sent back before.
  • An accessibility audit list of every finding — its rule and location, the before/after of any automatic fix, and who acknowledged it and when — so you can see exactly what the machine changed and who reviewed it.

From there you choose one of two actions:

  • Approve — clears it for publishing.
  • Request changes — sends it back to the author. A note explaining what needs to change is required, so the author knows what to fix.

The Public Newsletter Page

Every published newsletter has a stable web address — one URL families can open on any phone, tablet, or computer without signing in. Drafts are private: anyone who tries the address of an unpublished newsletter sees "not found."

Getting and sharing the link

  • Preview before publishing: In the editor, click Preview to open a view of the public page for your draft. Nothing is visible to families yet — this is for your review only.
  • After publishing: The editor shows a View published page link. Copy that address and paste it into ParentSquare, your school email, or any other communication tool you use to reach families.
  • Taking it offline: Mark the newsletter back to draft at any time; the public address returns "not found" until you publish again.

What families see

The published page uses a Smore-style layout: a bold colored banner at the top, colored section headers, and a photo-forward reading experience. It adapts automatically to any screen size — photos and galleries reflow on phones.

Every published page includes a reader-controls toolbar with:

  • Table of contents — jump to any section; works even with JavaScript turned off.
  • Text size — small, medium, or large; the choice is saved on the reader's device (nothing is sent to a server).
  • High-contrast mode — switches to a high-contrast color scheme for readers who need it; also saved on their device.
  • Translate — on-demand translation for families whose first language is not English.
  • Share — copies or shares the newsletter link.

All toolbar controls are fully keyboard accessible.

Image lightbox

Families can click (or press Enter) on any photo to see it at full size. Arrow keys move between images in a gallery; Esc closes the lightbox. The lightbox respects reduced-motion preferences for readers who find animation uncomfortable.

Flyers on the page

Styled-callout flyers render as accessible event cards with a real heading, the date, time, and location as text, and a call-to-action link. Uploaded flyers render as a two-column block — the flyer image (click to enlarge) beside the extracted text as real headings, lists, and links. Any link that opens in a new tab tells screen-reader users so, and link text is always descriptive — never "click here."

Analytics

Analytics are the reason most schools use a tool like this: they show how families are actually reading your newsletters. The dashboards are part of the in-app experience, so they load instantly and update as you change the time range — no page reloads. There are two dashboards.

  • View analytics for this newsletter — from the builder or the newsletter card. Shows how one specific issue is being read.
  • School analytics — from the Newsletters Home. Shows the combined picture across all of that school's newsletters.

A single toggle switches the whole dashboard between the last 7, 30, or 90 days. Each headline figure also shows a “vs previous period” trend so you can tell at a glance whether a number is climbing or slipping.

What the per-newsletter dashboard shows

  • Total views — how many times the page was opened.
  • Unique daily visitors — how many separate people opened it (counted once per day — see Privacy below).
  • Reading depth — how many readers scrolled to 25%, 50%, 75%, and 100% of the page, so you can see whether people read to the end.
  • Average time on page — roughly how long readers spent.
  • Device — the split between mobile, tablet, and desktop readers.
  • Where readers came from — whether they arrived from email, a website, social media, or a direct link.
  • Approximate location — the city or region readers opened it from (see the note below).
  • Read to the end — the share of readers who reached the bottom of the newsletter (this is the fourth headline figure on the dashboard).
  • Most-clicked links & flyers — which links and flyers got the most clicks, so you can see what families found most useful.

What the per-school dashboard shows

The school dashboard combines the totals across every newsletter for that school and adds a most-viewed leaderboard (each newsletter links straight to its own dashboard) so you can see which issues drew the most readers over time.

Accessible charts. Every chart carries the same numbers as real text — a screen-reader data table or a labelled legend — so nothing depends on seeing color, and each chart has a spoken summary. The time-range toggle, the up/down trend arrows, and all links work by keyboard and read correctly to assistive technology.

Today vs. earlier days

Today's numbers are live — they update as families read. Earlier days are finalized once nightly, so anything before today is a settled, stable number that won't keep changing.

Privacy by design. Analytics use no cookies and do no cross-day or cross-site tracking — a reader is never followed from one day or one site to another. A visitor is counted using a one-way daily code, so “unique visitors” means unique per day. The visitor's IP address and browser details are used for a split second to work out the device type and approximate city, then immediately discarded — never stored. No student personal data is ever collected.
Note on location: The approximate-location figures only appear if the server has the optional location database installed. If it isn't installed, that one table simply stays blank — every other analytics number is unaffected.

Quick Reference Card

Publish Workflow

  1. Sign in at newsletters.eusd.org
  2. Pick your school
  3. Start blank or duplicate last issue
  4. Add sections and content; run the AI alt-text and flyer-extract helpers
  5. Run Check & fix; acknowledge flags and resolve must-fix items
  6. Click Preview to review the public page
  7. Submit for review (gate confirms all items are resolved)
  8. An approver Approves (or Requests changes with a note)
  9. Once approved, click Publish
  10. Copy the public link; share via ParentSquare or email; check analytics later
